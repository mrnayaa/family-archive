December 31, 1988

This chapter summarizes how my parents met each other on New Year's Eve in 1988. Every year, my mom made it a tradition to attend a party at a university in Volgograd. In '88, she found herself at a discotheque thrown by the Kacha Aviation School, where my dad was pursuing dreams of becoming a pilot. All guests and students gathered in the assembly room to make new acquaintances, dance, and wait for the midnight chimes. Among the crowd, my dad spotted my mom on the dance floor, and from then on, they spent the night getting to know each other, dancing and celebrating New Year's.

My favorite part of the story is when my dad offered to walk my mom home. Despite being a student and technically restricted to campus grounds at night, he left out that he'd been expelled that morning. It was a time of change in the late '80s, with the USSR undergoing reforms, including downsizing the military. Many aspiring military pilots, including my dad, were expelled due to the state's shifting priorities.

However, a stroke of luck at the security post allowed my dad to accompany my mom home. When it was time to say good night, my mom gave him her phone number. She only said it once, and without pen or paper, my dad had to memorize the digits to call her and ask her on a date. To this day, whenever my dad recounts this story, he proudly recites my mom's old phone number:

390633
